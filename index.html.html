— Quick read

Born in Eluru, trained in Vadodara, building from London. AI engineer working with Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT — fluent in deep learning, CNNs, and the careful half of shipping software.

/ 01 Origin

Where I started

Eluru, Andhra Pradesh — a small town that taught me to be patient with hard problems. I picked up programming on a borrowed laptop and a slow connection. The constraint made me careful, and careful made me curious. By the time I got to Parul University in Vadodara, I had committed to AI as a craft, not a trend.

/ 02 Path

How I got here

Diploma in Computer Science → B.Tech in Artificial Intelligence → MSc Artificial Intelligence at the University of East London. Three programmes, three cities, six years working across hospitality, retail, kitchens, warehouses, and customer service desks in between. The non-AI work paid the bills, but it also taught me the soft half of building software — listening, reading rooms, and finishing what I started under pressure.

/ 03 Practice

How I work with AI

I treat the frontier models as a panel rather than a single oracle. I prototype on Claude when I need long-context reasoning, switch to Gemini when cost or vision matters, and reach for ChatGPT and Custom GPTs for fast tooling. I’m comfortable in PyTorch and TensorFlow for the deep-learning side — CNN classifiers, training loops, and evaluation harnesses are part of the same toolbox, not a separate skill.

/ 04 Outlook

What I’m looking for

An industrial placement, a part-time AI engineer role, or a research collaboration where the work goes into production and the team values careful thinking over fast slides. I prefer small teams, real users, and a boss who’d rather have one good answer than ten loud ideas.
